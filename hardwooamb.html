You successfully turned the Jin ambasador to your side, and in doing so gained even more invaluable knowledge of the inner workings of the dynasty. You decide next to launch a surprise attack in a mountain pass. However, the mountains are not like your homeland steppes, and present a challenge for your horse archers. You realize you need to change tactics.

Your horse archers are the backbone of your army, and have been the reason you have gotten this far. Trust their years of training and the tactics your generals have built around this unique advantage.

Give the order for your archers to dismount and fight on foot.

More Info

This surprise attack was actually an answer to a surpise attack being planned by the Jin, which Genghis Khan likely came to learn about from the numerous Jin defectors who had joined his cause. The location was chosen to mitigate the fact that the Jin forces outnumbered the Mongols about 10 to 1.
