You turn to the south and spit on the ground. "The emperor should be a man from the sky like me," you say, before turning north and riding off. For all intents and purposes, you have declared war on the Jin Dynasty.

Your next step is to call a kurultai. You discuss the circumstances with everyone and you pray to Tengri. Finally you decide -

"The Eternal Blue Sky has promised us victory and vengeance."

"We must not declare war. It is not ours' to fight."

More Info

The exact phrasing of this quote often differs. H. Desmond Martin has it as "I thought that the ruler of the Middle Kingdom must be from Heaven, Can he be a person of such weakness as the prince of Wei! Why should I Kowtow for him!" Whatever the exact quote, Genghis Khan clearly had no respect for the new emperor, who was known as a weak and indecisive ruler.
