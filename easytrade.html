You reluctantly decide to establish trade between your empire and the Mongols. However, soon your governor in the city of Otrar alerts you that there is a caravan of merchants numbering in the hundreds, primarily Muslim, from the Mongol Empire to formally establish the trade ties. A perfect opportunity to demonstrate your true feelings about this agreement has presented itself. How do you react?

Order your governor to give them a lavish welcome and bestow upon them gifts to take back to their homeland.

Tell your governor to accuse the merchants of spying and have them arrested.

More Info

According to The Rise of Chingis Khan and His Conquest of North China by H. Desmond Martin, "This" (the Mongol caravan) "was exceptionally large. Juwayni says that it included four hundred and fifty persons, all Moslems, while Juzjani relates that the merchandise – silk, targhu stuffs, furs, gold, and silver – were carried by as many as five hundred camels.”
